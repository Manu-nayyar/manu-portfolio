The Challenge

When I joined Smart Food Safe in March 2023, the website had virtually zero organic search presence. The company was entirely dependent on paid advertising for web traffic and lead generation — an expensive and unsustainable channel for a B2B SaaS business targeting the food safety and compliance industry.

The goal was clear: build an organic search engine that could generate qualified B2B leads at a fraction of the cost of paid ads, and do it sustainably over time.

  • 0 organic clicks, 0 impressions, no keyword rankings
  • 100% reliance on paid advertising for traffic and leads
  • No content strategy, no technical SEO foundation
  • Highly specialised B2B niche with long sales cycles
  • Website with significant technical SEO issues including slow load times and poor Core Web Vitals scores

The Approach

Rather than chasing quick wins, I approached SmartFoodSafe.com like a product — building a durable organic channel through a phased strategy that addressed technical foundations first, then content, then authority.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–4)

Technical SEO Overhaul

  • Full technical audit: crawl errors, redirect chains, duplicate content, canonical issues
  • Core Web Vitals optimisation — improved LCP, CLS, and FID scores to pass Google thresholds
  • Site architecture redesign for crawlability and internal linking
  • Schema markup implementation across product, FAQ, and article pages
  • Page speed optimisation: image compression, lazy loading, caching, CDN setup
Phase 2 — Content Engine (Months 3–12)

Keyword Research & Content Strategy

  • Deep keyword research across food safety, compliance, and HACCP topics
  • Mapped 300+ target keywords across the buyer journey (awareness → consideration → decision)
  • Built a content calendar producing 4–6 high-quality articles per month
  • Developed topic clusters to build topical authority in food safety compliance
  • Optimised existing pages for featured snippets and People Also Ask results
  • Created bottom-of-funnel comparison and use-case pages targeting high-intent searches
Phase 3 — Scale & Conversion (Year 2–3)

Authority Building & CRO

  • Led link building outreach — guest posts, digital PR, and industry directory submissions
  • Conversion rate optimisation on landing pages, increasing Calendly booking rate by 76%
  • GA4 + GTM implementation for granular funnel tracking and attribution
  • AI-search optimisation (structured data, authoritative entity signals) driving 177% growth in ChatGPT and Gemini referral traffic
  • Managed a 7-member team across content writing, design, development, and outreach
Key principle: every decision had to serve both the user and the search engine. Content that didn't genuinely help food safety professionals didn't get published — this built the trust signals that accelerated rankings.

Results

After 3 years of consistent execution, the results speak clearly. Smart Food Safe now operates a full organic acquisition engine that generates more leads than paid ads — at a 74% lower cost.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced that B2B SaaS SEO requires patience and a clear funnel strategy. Quick-win tactics don't build durable channels — systematic content production mapped to buyer intent does.

What drove the most impact

  • Technical SEO first — no amount of content helps if Google can't crawl and index effectively
  • Topic authority over isolated articles — clusters outperformed standalone pages by a wide margin
  • Conversion-optimised landing pages — traffic means nothing without lead capture
  • AI-search readiness — structured data and authoritative entity signals are becoming table stakes

What I'd do differently

  • Start link-building outreach in month 1, not month 6 — backlinks are a slow-moving lever
  • Implement GA4 funnel tracking from day one, not after 3 months of blind growth
  • Involve the product team earlier for use-case and solution pages
